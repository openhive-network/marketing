Buttcoins: 

How can we better support community-driven projects and initiatives?

- allow projects to apply for small SOS initiatives.  Ie, a community has a contest or a challenge idea, they can create a proposal requesting aide for prize money.  A small portion of Value Plans budget could serve as an incubator for small initiatives and community drives.
peer to peer onboarding mentorship program. Verified Hivians onboarding support network. A respected Hivian can submit a new onboarded user to receive a 1 year support and guidance trial. Both mentor and new Hivian create a feedback loop to collect data on any success or failure.
What recourses or tools do community members need to succeed?

- a search function that works
- easier onboarding tools
- more guidance for new users
- easier avenues to find high quality content
- Hive Meetup starter kits

I think we should have the right to block an account from engaging with us, not to block the account from others, but if someone feels threatened, abused or trolled by another account, there should be a way to block that account from being able to engage with your personal account. I see this as a form of sovereignty over your account. This is not about banning or removing an account from Hive, it about the right to block an account from interacting with you directly.

(I’m aware of several men and women who have left the blockchain because they felt threatened. To succeed, Hive must develop tools to foster safety for all its users while simultaneously allowing all accounts to conduct themselves as they see fit. (Downvoting and Muting is not adequate))
