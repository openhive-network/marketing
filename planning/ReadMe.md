# **Value Plan-Specific Section**

# 1. Process

## Project Planning

- Draft a project plan/idea and present it in writing to any Keyholder or Project Manager (they will direct you)
- Work with the assigned project manager to create a timeline and a budget idea
- Create a spreadsheet with all the outlined costs and send it to your project manager
- Work with the project manager to finalize the budget
- Fill out the SMART goals spreadsheet if applicable
- Connect with one of the professional graphic designers on Hive to create visual elements for the project
- Speak to the vendors where possible and onboard them to Hive so they may receive payment for their goods/services directly
- Collect any legal documentation or contracts and send them to your project manager 
- Process funds as necessary to ensure all vendors and service providers are paid
- Carry out the event and have fun 
- Document the event in social media, on chain, or otherwise as applicable for the event
- Save all your receipts and documents and file them as specified below

# 2. Definitions

## Hive "Merch"

- Promotional or event-specific items with Hive branding
- Designed by a professional designer on Hive where possible
- Pre-approved as designated for a specific event or use
- Printed only by using vetted, good quality printers
- Has a pre-approved cost allocated to it
- Focuses on Hive but may have a modified logo that is thematic or be double-branded

### Damaged Merch

- Merch that is printed and is damaged due to unforeseen circumstances such as weather cannot be used as part of events or activities. It may be given out to people at informal settings so it does not go to waste.
- Merch that is so poorly made that it skews the Hive logo and its colors beyond reason is to be recycled where possible. 
- Merch that is left over from older events (has the name of a sponsored conference or a date that passed) is to be reused at activities where it is not of central focus.

### Vendor Approval

- Must be a real business, not a home hobbyist (cannot be a person who irons on logos on shirts as a hobby or a side gig)
- Priority should be given to local businesses to align with Hive's grassroots vision
- Must have the ability to write an invoice and/or provide a receipt
- Must deliver a quality product (vendors who fail to deliver are not to be used after the failure has occurred)

### Accuracy 

- Merch quality is paramount and designs must be checked ahead of time in order to ensure they are accurate 
- Common mistakes including using non-existent or defunct dapp logos, adding incorrect URLs, or calling Hive "Hive Blog" 

## Events

- Please use the above sheet for suitable events 
- Events need to have a run-up time to allow for sufficient planning
- All events are non-profit (cannot be used in conjunction for personal benefits or business ventures)
- Where possible, funds are to be send straight to vendors to allow for transparent accounting on their part and eas their tax reporting obligations
- Any left over funds or funds gained as a result of the event are to be sent to either the Value Plan account or the DHF, as appropriate

### Meetups

- Made of community members and maybe some external guests
- Are informal and mostly about socializing
- Food and logistics are NOT covered as everyone is ordering their own meal
- No speaker slots and no per diem
- No insurance
- Hive merch YES covered
- Must use the red 'Meetup' design (activities that do not use the design will not qualify as Meetups)

### Tech Events

- Made up of external participants mostly
- Organized with partners who are paying for a portion of the event
- Are formal and are about learning and networking
- Sometimes have sponsorships
- Per diem and transportation are covered
- No insurance on our end but partner should have it
- Hive merch YES covered

### Workshops

- Made up of a few Hive members and a sizable group of external participants
- Organized by ourselves
- Are formal and are about learning about Hive or some service/dapp such as Distriator
- Per diem and transportation are covered within reason
- Schedule of work must be such as to add up to a day and warrant per diem
- Insurance provided by the venue
- Only a few pieces of Hive merch YES covered

### Conferences (Sponsored)

- Well-organized professional large events made up of external participants and guests
- Sponsored with a speaker spot and/or booth
- Very formal with select team of Hive members representing
- Per diem, transport and accommodations included as necessary
- Full day of work expected as necessary for the event
- Travel insurance sometimes provided as needed
- Hive merch YES covered
- Design work YES covered

### Conferences (Attended)

- Hive members who have 1. experience with conferences and representing Hive 2. the opportunity attending conferences we are NOT speaking at or sponsoring in any way
- One or two members per event
- Event tickets (regular level ticket, only if affordable), very minor transportation and per diem covered
- Hive merch YES covered for the attendee to wear
- No insurance or any other costs covered

# 3. Project Management 

## Records

### Receipt and Document Management

- Create a Google Drive or equivalent preferred repository
- Create folders for your managed project
- Add new folders as new events are organized
- Place spreadsheet of costs for the event into each folders
- Take photo of receipt and invoice and place into event folders
- Maintain this system for every single spreadsheet, receipt and invoice
- If there is no receipt, such as for cash transactions, write down in a document what was spent in cash and place into folders

Be sure to avoid accidentally overwriting older sheets by accident by ensuring that you are replicating sheets when using one for a template of another. 

### Credit Cards

If you have a credit card with a large limit and can put your flights or merch purchases on it, for vendors who don't yet accept HBD, by all means. Or if you want to pay for taxis and then pull up a statement. If you don't have a credit card, you don't need to front any funds. The funds are provided ahead of time. The only funds that may need to go through the refund system are if there's an unforeseen expense and you covered it on the spot or when we have last-minute team roster changes in large conferences and per diems are unclear. There is no expectation that all funds are to be fronted.

### Per diems

Per diems are funds to ensure everyone has three sufficient meals during a full day of volunteering for Hive. Per diems are not meant to reflect local restaurant prices for all means but be a combination of food purchases from different sources.

- 30 HBD per day 
- Only issued for event days/full days of work
- Only issued to individuals who have a pre-determined role at an activity or event (cannot show up unexpectedly)
- Not issued for activities where food is provided in other ways
- Not issued for long-term projects that require irregular time commitments 

## Profits 

All profits, including those made from ticket sales or those made as a result of onboarding a vendor (Distriator), need to be sent back. 

### Return to DHF
- Profits that were made as a result of the event but not directly related to it
- Vendor-transactional or referral gains/pay funds that were gained as a result of the event
- Remaining side funds paid by a Hive dapp receiving/not receiving DHF funding

### Return to Value Plan
- Ticket sales from ticketed events 
- Monetary gifts and grants gained from events or engagements 
- Any funds that were issued and are left over for whatever reason (contingency funds, unused portions due to cost savings)
